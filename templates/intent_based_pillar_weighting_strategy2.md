## Purpose of This Document

Not all content serves the same purpose.

A technical instruction manual, a research paper, a founder story, and a commercial landing page require different structural and ethical priorities.

A common mistake in optimization is attempting to maximize every signal equally across all content types.

This leads to:

- Structural confusion
- Reduced semantic clarity
- Weak human engagement
- Misaligned AI interpretation
- Ethical risk from inappropriate persuasion

Intent-Based Pillar Weighting solves this problem by aligning pillar emphasis with content purpose.

---

# The Core Principle

The 7 Pillars remain constant.

The **weight assigned to each pillar varies depending on intent.**

The practitioner selects a Mode before drafting begins.

Modes provide strategic alignment between:

- User expectations
- Algorithmic classification
- Communication objectives
- Ethical boundaries

---

# Why This Matters in 2026

Modern search and generative systems classify content by intent signals:

- Informational
- Transactional
- Conversational
- Narrative
- Commercial
- Educational

Mixed signals reduce trust.

For example:

- A page pretending to be neutral while selling aggressively
- A research article containing marketing language
- A tutorial written like a personal story

Intent-Based Weighting prevents these conflicts.

---

# The Four Primary Modes

The Practitioner Layer defines four operational modes:

1. **Authority Mode**
2. **Utility Mode**
3. **Narrative Mode**
4. **Commercial Mode**

Each mode shifts pillar emphasis intentionally.

---

# Mode 1: Authority Mode

---

## Purpose

Explain reality, establish expertise, or define concepts with high credibility requirements.

Used for:

- White papers
- Research publications
- Legal explanations
- Technical frameworks
- Educational authority content

# Example Application Across One Organization

The same company may operate in multiple modes simultaneously.

---

## Example 1: Knowledge Base Article (Utility Mode)

Goal: Reset user password.

Execution:

- Immediate BLUF answer
- Numbered steps
- No narrative storytelling
- Minimal contextual explanation

Primary pillars: GEO, AIO, SEO.

---

## Example 2: Founder Origin Story (Narrative Mode)

Goal: Build emotional connection.

Execution:

- Story-driven structure
- Personal reflections
- Human pacing
- Transparent framing as subjective experience

Primary pillars: NLO, HEO, EEO.

---

## Example 3: Industry Compliance Report (Authority Mode)

Goal: Establish expertise and credibility.

Execution:

- Definitions and citations
- Raw data evidence packs
- Methodology disclosure
- Boundary clarification

Primary pillars: SDO, EEO, HEO.

---

# Cross-Mode Consistency Rule

While pillar weights change, the framework itself does not.

Modes influence **execution**, not **principles**.

---

# The HEO Veto (Universal Constant)

Human Ethics Optimization (HEO) applies across all modes.

If content:

- Manipulates fear
- Uses deceptive claims
- Violates audience agency
- Misrepresents capability
- Conceals limitations

The content fails Practitioner standards.

It does not ship.

No performance metric overrides HEO.
