## Pattern 1: The Semantic Boundary Box Strategy

**The Tension:** Traditional marketing and legacy SEO taught creators to cast the widest net possible to capture all related search traffic. However, in an AI-mediated ecosystem, failing to define what your concept is *not* allows generative parsers (GEO) to dilute your proprietary framework. AI models synthesize aggressively; if you do not establish rigid conceptual limits, the AI will hallucinate false capabilities or blend your unique methodology with generic industry advice, undermining your Semantic Depth (SDO).

**The Solution:** Explicitly defining the negative space of a concept by inserting a "Boundary Box" that sets strict rules for how the information should be categorized by machines.

## Pattern 2: The Evidence Pack Embedding Strategy

**The Tension:** Claiming authority (legacy SEO) versus proving authority (EEO). Proving expertise is incredibly difficult today because Large Language Models can generate perfectly articulate, confident, and "expert-sounding" text in seconds. Relying on an author bio at the bottom of a page is no longer enough to establish trust. If you only provide text, you look like an AI.

**The Solution:** The Evidence Pack strategy forces the practitioner to embed raw, verifiable artifacts of lived experience directly into the narrative flow (NLO). This creates a "trust moat" that generative AI cannot fabricate, fulfilling the Experience & Expertise Optimization (EEO) requirement.
