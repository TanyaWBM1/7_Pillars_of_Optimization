## Purpose of This Pattern

Intent-Based Pillar Weighting teaches practitioners **how to apply the 7 Pillars proportionally** based on the purpose of the content.

The framework is **not a checklist where every pillar receives equal weight at all times.**

Instead, practitioners intentionally emphasize certain pillars depending on:

- Content intent
- Audience context
- Risk level
- Mode of communication
- Desired outcome

This approach prevents mechanical optimization and preserves strategic intelligence.

---

## The Core Cross-Pillar Tension

Many optimization systems fail because they assume:

> â€œMore optimization everywhere equals better results.â€

This is false.

Different contexts require different pillar emphasis.

For example:

| Scenario | Dominant Need |
|----------|---------------|
Technical documentation | Machine clarity |
Storytelling | Human resonance |
Legal analysis | Precision and ethics |
Product marketing | Trust and persuasion |
Educational material | Comprehension and structure |

Applying equal pillar weight in all cases creates friction.

---

## The Strategic Solution

The practitioner operates using **Modes**.

Each Mode represents a different balance of pillar emphasis.

The pillars remain constant.

Only their **weighting changes**.

---

## The Three Primary Modes

The Practitioner Layer defines three foundational modes:

1. Utility Mode  
2. Narrative Mode  
3. Authority Mode  

These modes cover most real-world communication scenarios.
